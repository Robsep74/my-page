Overview:

Color Theory is the study of how colors interact, how they are perceived, and how they can be combined effectively. It is used in art, design, and marketing to create visually appealing compositions and convey emotions.

Here are 20 key color theory terms along with their definitions:

1- Primary Colors

  • Red, Blue, Yellow - The three colors that cannot be created by mixing other colors.

2- Secondary Colors.

  • Green, Violet, Orange - are formed by mixing two primary colors.
  • Green: Mix yellow and blue in equal parts.
  • Violet: Mix red and blue in equal parts.
  • Orange: Mix red and yellow in equeal parts.

3- Tertiary Colors.

  • Tertiary colors result from mixing a primary color with a secondary color. The 6 tertiary colors, along with their mixing formulas, are:
  1. Blue-violet: Mix blue and violet in equal parts.
  2. Blue-green: Mix blue and green in equal parts.
  3. Yellow-green: Mix yellow and green in equal parts.
  4. Yellow-orange: Mix yellow and orange in equal parts.
  5. Red-orange: Mix red and orange in equal parts.
  6. Red-violet: Mix red and violet in equal parts.

4- Hues.

  • Hues are often defined as the purest form of a color. There are 12 that are considered to be the purest and most vivid. The three primary colors red, blue, and yellow. The three secondary colors green, violet, and orange. The six tertiary colors:
  1. Blue-violet.
  2. Blue-green.
  3. Yellow-green.
  4. Yellow-orange.
  5. Red-orange.
  6. Red-violet.
